# Interlude: East Meets West — Castells vs Chaoyang

> *Human towers exist in two corners of the world: Catalonia and Malaysia. One is a civic tradition of national pride. The other is a spiritual practice of faith and discipleship.*

## A Tale of Two Traditions

| Dimension | Castells (Catalonia) | 朝阳体操队 (Malaysia) |
|-----------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Origin** | 18th century folk tradition | 1976 Buddhist cultural group |
| **Max Height** | 10 levels | 6 levels (4 in performance) |
| **Base Structure** | Large *pinya* (base), hundreds of people | 3–5 person base circles |
| **Music** | *Gralla* (traditional wind instrument) | Integrated songs with choreography |
| **Philosophy** | Civic pride, national identity | 师弟不二 (Master-Disciple Bond), 异体同心 (Different Bodies, One Heart) |
| **Training** | Weekly neighborhood practice | Faith-grounded, 5 AM *gongyo* before sessions |
| **Clothing** | White pants, colored shirt, black sash | Coordinated performance costumes |
| **Purpose** | Cultural expression, competition | Cultural dissemination, *kosen-rufu*, human transformation |
| **Recognition** | UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage (2010) | National government recognition |
| **Peak Event** | Tarragona competition (biennial) | SEA Games 1989 (19 towers) |

## What Chaoyang Shares with Castells

1. **The Tower as Unity** — both traditions use the physical structure of a human tower to represent collective achievement
2. **Trust is Structural** — in both, the base must trust the climbers, climbers must trust the base. One failure affects all.
3. **Training Culture** — both require dedicated, repeated practice. You don't just "try" a tower.
4. **Intergenerational Transfer** — knowledge passes from senior to junior, master to apprentice
5. **Community Identity** — membership in a *colla* or chapter shapes personal identity

## What Makes Chaoyang Unique

While Castells is fundamentally a civic tradition, Chaoyang adds a **spiritual dimension** that makes it historically unique:

1. **Faith as Foundation** — training begins with *gongyo*; *Daimoku* is chanted throughout
2. **The Tower as Treasure Tower** — the tower symbolizes the *Treasure Tower of the Gohonzon*, not just human achievement
3. **Master-Disciple Relationship** — the vertical structure of the tower mirrors the vertical bond between mentor and disciple
4. **Human Revolution** — the purpose is not performance alone, but individual character transformation
5. **Peace Mission** — performances serve a larger mission of peace and cultural exchange
