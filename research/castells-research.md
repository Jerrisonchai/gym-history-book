# Castells Research — Catalan Human Towers

> Source: Wikipedia + UNESCO ICH
> Date: 2026-06-26
> Purpose: Comparative analysis with 朝阳体操队 for documentary book

## Quick Facts

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Origin | Valls, Catalonia, Spain — first documented 1712 |
| Name meaning | "Castell" = Castle (Catalan) |
| UNESCO Status | Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity (2010) |
| Current scale | 99 registered teams (colles) as of 2015 |
| Max height | 10 levels ("4 de 10") |
| Global exposure | 1992 Barcelona Olympics opening ceremony |

## Tower Structure

```
         enxaneta (child, raises hand at top)
              |
        pom de dalt (top 3 levels — young children)
              |
           tronc (trunk — 2-5 heavier men per level)
              |
           pinya (base — hundreds, anyone can join)
```

- **Pinya**: The massive base. Acts as safety net if tower collapses. Open to anyone.
- **Tronc**: Vertical core. Heavier, stronger members. Bears most weight.
- **Pom de dalt**: Children form the top levels. Light enough for higher tiers.
- **Enxaneta**: The child who crowns the tower, raises hand to signal completion.

## Core Values

1. **Força (Strength)** — Physical and mental. The base bears massive weight.
2. **Equilibri (Balance)** — Each person depends on everyone else.
3. **Valor (Courage)** — Climbing higher means greater risk.
4. **Seny (Common Sense/Wisdom)** — Knowing when to push and when to stop.
5. **Solidaritat (Solidarity)** — Rich and poor, young and old, all in the same pinya.
6. **Intergenerational Transmission** — Knowledge passed from elders to youth through practice only.

## Key Rituals

- **Toc de Castells**: Traditional music (gralla wind instrument) sets rhythm
- **Faixa (sash)**: Black sash protects lower back, serves as handhold/foot hold
- **Team colors**: Each colla has unique shirt color (identity)
- **Dismantling**: Done in reverse order — often the most dangerous phase
- **Square tradition**: Performed in town square, in front of town hall balcony

## Comparison Points with 朝阳体操队

| Dimension | Castells | 朝阳体操队 |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **Origin** | 1712, folk festival | 1976, SGM cultural performance |
| **Peak scale** | 10 levels | 5-6 layers (五层塔/六层塔) |
| **Largest event** | Barcelona Olympics 1992 | SEA Games 1989 (19 towers!) |
| **Participants** | Hundreds in pinya | 1500 (1985), 2200 (1989) |
| **Music** | Gralla + Toc de Castells | Song-integrated (music-driven performances) |
| **Women** | Included since 1980s | Male division only (男子部) |
| **Children** | Enxaneta at top | N/A |
| **Spiritual** | Civic/folk tradition | Buddhist (Soka Gakkai), master-disciple bond |
| **Philosophy** | Seny (practical wisdom) | 异体同心 (different bodies, one heart) |
| **Knowledge transfer** | Generational within team | Seniors teach juniors; VHS recordings shared nationwide |
| **Naming** | — | Named "朝阳" (Morning Sun) by Ikeda Sensei, 2004 |
| **UNESCO** | YES (2010) | Not registered |
