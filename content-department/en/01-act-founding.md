# Act I: Founding (1976–1990)

## 1.1 Origin — 1976

The SGM Gymnastics Troupe began in 1976, when a group of over twenty pioneering members attended a cultural festival performance. From this starting point, they set their sights on the 21st century and established the gymnastics troupe — turning a new page in the history of *kosen-rufu* (world peace through individual happiness).

## 1.2 The First Tower — 1982

In 1982, the entire Culture Department nationwide mobilized for the very first time to perform at the First Cultural Festival. Three hundred gymnastic troupe members participated in the performance. The greatest challenge was the **five-level tower** — nobody had any prior experience whatsoever.

But all members embodied the spirit of **Different Bodies, One Heart** (异体同心): undaunted by hardship, persevering relentlessly. When they fell, they rose up again and resumed practice. On the day itself, they successfully erected the tower — it was the most outstanding and unforgettable program of the Cultural Festival. At the same time, they created new history for *kosen-rufu* in Malaysia.

## 1.3 Knowledge Transfer — 1983

In 1983, several Japanese gymnastics troupe leaders were invited to Malaysia for exchange meetings and training seminars. They transferred safety protocols and fundamental techniques to us — a transmission that deepened our sense of mission toward the gymnastics troupe.

## 1.4 Expansion — 1985

On April 27–28, 1985, the Second Cultural Festival was held at the National Stadium in Kuala Lumpur. The number of performers nationwide had doubled compared to the first festival. Gymnastics troupe members increased to **1,500**, with proportionally greater challenges and more obstacles.

Yet for the sake of the festival's success, every member resolved to overcome difficulties, grounded in the *Gohonzon* and manifesting the power of *Daimoku* (chanting Nam-myoho-renge-kyo). The five-level tower was successfully erected once again — propelling the Second Cultural Festival to even greater heights and opening wider the path of *kosen-rufu*.

## 1.5 Youth Steps Forward — 1986

On June 14–15, 1986, the First Youth Peace Culture Festival was held in Penang. This symbolized the Youth Division taking its first steps on the cultural stage for *kosen-rufu*. The youth performed with the fierce struggle of lions, igniting the passion and power of young people toward the mission of 21st-century *kosen-rufu*, contributing to the construction of a peaceful, prosperous, and stable society.

## 1.6 University Recognition — 1987

On September 5, 1987, the University of Malaya hosted a "Martial Arts Night," and the gymnastics troupe was honored to be invited to present a program. One hundred eighty members demonstrated discipline and the spirit of *Different Bodies, One Heart*. Their vigorous and spirited performance captured hearts and earned widespread acclaim.

This significant performance aligned perfectly with Ikeda Sensei's vision: *"Through education and culture, build bonds of friendship between people and national prosperity."* It also established a bridge of friendship between Soka Gakkai and the University of Malaya.

*(Sensei, in his capacity as founder of Soka University, visited Malaysia for the first time from February 5–8, 1988, for educational and cultural exchange.)*

## 1.7 National Momentum — 1988

On July 30–31, 1988, the Second Youth Peace Culture Festival was held in Ipoh. To celebrate the nation's 31st year of independence, the theme was *"Youth's New Century Departure — Sailing Toward the Peace Journey of 2001."* One hundred ninety gymnastic troupe members performed with vitality, courageous effort, and *Daimoku*, ensuring the festival's success and reaffirming the mission of 21st-century *kosen-rufu*.

## 1.8 The Rain Miracle — 1989

On July 29–30, 1989, the Third Youth Peace Culture Festival was held in Johor. It rained heavily for two consecutive days, leaving the ground wet. Even on the performance day, the rain continued — **they performed in the rain** — and the wet floor became extremely slippery.

But nobody lost heart. Instead, five hundred members held firm conviction, with faith and *Daimoku* as the foundation. They broke through every obstacle, displaying swift, resolute movements filled with vitality and the spirit of *Different Bodies, One Heart*. The entire performance achieved great success — unprecedented in scale.

The Chief Director afterward told everyone: *"This Cultural Festival is the most unforgettable, most successful, most memorable — it has created historical proof."*

## 1.9 The Defining Moment — SEA Games 1989

On August 20, 1989, Malaysia hosted the 15th Southeast Asian Games. The organization was honored to be invited by the government to present *human characters* and *mechanical gymnastics* performances at the opening ceremony.

The SEA Games preparation took approximately one year: **2,200 men** were mobilized nationwide irrespective of age. In Kuala Lumpur alone, about 700 men's division members were gathered. With only four months remaining for combined practice, training was held every Sunday and public holiday.

**Challenges faced:**
- Illness and fatigue
- Insufficient attendance numbers
- Food and logistics
- Communication barriers (pre-internet era — formations were described with paper and pen, mailed to state chapters)
- Coordinators traveled nationwide to prepare the performance

Remarkably, nationwide members held great faith, continuously supporting us with *Daimoku*. The Chief Director set the slogan: **"Success! Success! Great Success! Victory! Victory! Great Victory!"**

Every leader and member grounded themselves in *Daimoku*, rising at 5 AM for morning *gongyo* (prayer) before each practice, maintaining this all the way to the performance day. The biggest challenge was formations — the four-level tower failed in every rehearsal.

On the day itself, they fulfilled the slogan exactly: overcoming all problems, defeating every obstacle, successfully completing **19 four-level towers** — a great success and great victory! Every participant engraved an indelible golden memory in their lives.

This was not only a new chapter for *kosen-rufu* but also the highest honor for the nation. It became the largest achievement of the 1980s–90s, uniting men's division members nationwide, consolidating gymnastic troupe members across the country. **August 20, 1989, became Gymnastics Troupe Day.**

## 1.10 Government Recognition — Q Project

On November 27, 1989, the government held the *Q Project* launch ceremony at the National Stadium. The gymnastics troupe was once again invited to perform. Three hundred fifty members delivered the performance after just 20 days of training — aligned with the national objective of nurturing outstanding human talent.

## 1.11 National Organization — 1990

On May 24–25, 1990, the Fourth Youth Peace Culture Festival was hosted by the Kuantan chapter. The theme: *"Youth! Leap onto the Stage of the 21st Century."* Twelve hundred youth division members performed, overcoming insufficient numbers and various difficulties with unwavering resolve.

**On August 20, 1990, the SGM Gymnastics Troupe was officially established as a national organization.**

*This concludes Act I — the founding era. From twenty pioneers to a national force of thousands, the foundation had been laid.*
