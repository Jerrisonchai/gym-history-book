# Preface: Why This Book Exists

> *"Success! Success! Great Success! Victory! Victory! Great Victory!"*
> — Chief Director, 1989 SEA Games

This is not a typical sports history book.

This is the story of Malaysian-Chinese youth, grounded in the faith of Soka Gakkai, who built human towers with their own bodies. From twenty-plus pioneers in 1976 to 2,200 men nationwide completing nineteen four-level towers at the 1989 SEA Games — this is not competitive athletics, but a spiritual practice driven by faith, unity, and the master-disciple bond.

For fifty years, the Chaoyang Gymnastics Team (formerly: SGM Gymnastics Troupe) has been building human towers across Malaysia. Every level is a manifestation of trust — those at the base bearing those above, coaches believing in members, members believing in their mentor. This trust transcends race, age, and language.

**The purpose of this book:**

1. **Record History** — Preserve every significant event, key figure, and tower story from five decades
2. **Transmit Spirit** — Pass the spirit of *Different Bodies, One Heart* (异体同心) and *Master-Disciple Bond* (师弟不二) to new generations
3. **Educate the Public** — Showcase a unique form of Malaysian cultural exchange to the world
4. **Build Bridges** — Position Chaoyang within the global human tower tradition through comparison with Catalan Castells

This is a book about human towers. More importantly, it's a book about *people* — those who climbed in the rain, stood back up after falling, and found strength in their faith.

**Dedicated to every brother who has ever climbed this tower.**
