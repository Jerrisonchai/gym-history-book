## Simulation Prompt: Iterative Human Tower Construction

### Phase 1: Unit Definition

Generate a single, schematic 3D human figure. This should be a basic, stylized mannequin with:
- Simple round head
- Columnar body
- Simple cylinder limbs for hands and legs
- Clear visual differentiation between front and back (e.g., simple face outline or texture change on front vs. plain back)

### Phase 2: Initial Assembly (2-Level Tower)

Multiply the figure defined in Phase 1 to create an initial tower structure:
- **Level 1 (Base):** Exactly 3 figures standing in a circle, oriented inward so they all face the center
- **Level 2:** 2 figures standing on the shoulders of the base circle

### Phase 3: Progressive Growth Simulation (3-Level and Beyond)

Continue the simulation by iteratively increasing the tower's height:
- Generate a sequence showing: 3-level tower → 4-level tower → continuing progression
- Each new level uses the exact figure type from Phase 1
- Apply the established stacking logic to grow the vertical column
- Maintain structural consistency throughout the sequence

## Notes

- These 3D models will serve as the primary visual identity of the book
- The progressive tower sequence mirrors the historical growth: 1982 (first 5-layer tower) → 1985 (1500-person tower) → 1989 (19 four-layer towers at SEA Games)
- Figures should evoke the "朝阳" (Morning Sun) spirit — bright, rising, aspirational
